## IV. Block 1 — The Thesis (8:00 – 25:00)

**Q1.1.** *Host:* You're framing this as "DeFi's next bottleneck isn't liquidity — it's settlement." Walk me through why that's the right way to look at it.

**Answer beat:** Existing DeFi assumed a human behind the wallet. Agents can't improvise. Need deterministic settlement. The bottleneck moves from "is there liquidity available" to "can value clear between non-human counterparties without arbitration."

**Q1.2.** *Host:* Skeptics will say this is just AI agent buzz on top of a DeFi project. What do you tell them?

**Answer beat:** Fair starting point. Reply with stack-already-exists, didn't repackage, recognized larger market. Acknowledge skepticism — don't fight it.

**Q1.3.** *Host:* The DeFAI category — who else is credibly in it, and where do you sit relative to them?

**Answer beat:** Olas (coordination), Virtuals (issuance velocity), Fetch / ASI (deployment surface), Giza (verifiable ML). Astarter at settlement layer underneath. Comparative naming = analyst credibility.

**Q1.4.** *Host:* What does "settlement layer" actually mean in practice?

**Answer beat:** Agent-to-agent payment without custodian, chargeback, or arbitration. Money Market for agent credit. DePIN payment medium. Governance for protocol parameters. Concrete, not abstract.

**Q1.5.** *Host:* If you're right about this, what does the agent economy look like in five years?

**Answer beat:** Majority of on-chain transactions originate from non-human actors. Settlement infrastructure becomes more important than any individual model or framework. The projects that win are the ones whose rails are already standing.
