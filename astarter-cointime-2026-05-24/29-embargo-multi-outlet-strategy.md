# Embargo + Multi-Outlet Drop Strategy

**The difference between agency-junior PR and category-defining PR.** A single send to a single outlet caps reach at that outlet's audience. A coordinated multi-outlet drop with one exclusive embargo can deliver 5–10x the impressions and triple the message penetration.

---

## I. The strategic logic

### Why embargo works

- Tier-1 outlets (CoinDesk / The Block / Bloomberg) won't run a story they didn't originate. They'll happily run an exclusive **first look** that gives them a 4–24 hour head start.
- The embargo creates *editorial value*: the outlet feels they got something nobody else has.
- After embargo lifts, every other outlet has a permission structure to follow ("CoinDesk reported today..." or "First reported by..."). This compounds reach via second-wave coverage.

### Why broad drop alone fails

- A press release sent simultaneously to 50 outlets goes into 50 inboxes that all assume someone else will write it. None of them feel special. Most ignore.
- Without a Tier-1 anchor, second-wave outlets have nothing to cite, so they don't pick up.

### Why exclusive forever (single outlet, no drop) fails

- One article = one audience. No second-wave amplification.
- Other outlets feel snubbed and may go cold on future pitches.
- Long-tail SEO is concentrated in one URL instead of distributed.

---

## II. The recommended structure

**Three-tier release:**

### Tier 1 — Embargoed exclusive (T-24h to T-0)
- **One** Tier-1 outlet gets the package under embargo
- Embargo language is explicit and signed (informal email reply counts)
- They publish at T-0
- We do **nothing** for that outlet's audience until T-0

### Tier 2 — Coordinated broad drop (T-0)
- At the moment Tier-1 publishes, drop to all Tier 2 outlets simultaneously
- Include Tier-1 article as reference: *"CoinDesk reports today on Astarter's repositioning..."*
- Provide full media kit, pre-approved quotables, hi-res assets

### Tier 3 — Second-wave amplification (T+1 to T+7)
- Outreach to Tier-3 and KOL list with: "Here's what's been picked up so far" + the original assets
- Most secondary outlets will republish if multiple Tier-2s have already run

**Subject:** "Exclusive: Astarter × UXLINK Web 4.0 settlement-layer alliance — first look offered"

> Hi [Editor],
>
> Quick note — we're announcing Astarter's strategic alliance with UXLINK on [date] under the framing of "settlement infrastructure for the autonomous AI agent economy." Original data analysis piece included (8 charts, primary-source citations).
>

>
> Materials available:
> - Long-form thesis (~1,850 words)

>
> If interested, I'll send the full package immediately under embargo. If not, no harm — happy to include you in the broader drop.
>
> Available for a 10-minute call this afternoon if useful.
>
> [Sign]

## V. Embargo terms (informal but explicit)

If editor says yes, follow up with:

> Great — sharing the full package now under embargo until [Date, Time, UTC].
>
> Embargo terms:
> 1. No reference to Astarter, UXLINK, or any details from this package before [embargo lift time]
> 2. We will not provide any of this material to other outlets until 60 minutes after your piece runs
> 3. Standard fact-check questions welcomed any time before publication
> 4. If embargo is broken inadvertently, please flag immediately so we can adjust the broader drop timing
>
> Anything we can do to make your write-up easier, just ask.

## VI. Anti-patterns to avoid

| Mistake | Why it kills the launch |
|---|---|
| Offering embargo to multiple outlets simultaneously | They find out from each other and pull. Trust burned. |
| Setting embargo time during a major market event | Your story dies under volatility coverage. Check the macro calendar. |
| Setting embargo for Friday afternoon | Lowest news traffic of the week. Use Tuesday 09:00 ET or Wednesday 09:00 ET. |
| Sending broad drop *before* the Tier-1 publishes | Tier-1 will pull. You lose the anchor. |
| Forgetting CN coordination | CN media operate on a separate clock — coordinate with WeChat/CN channels separately. |
| Reusing the same headline across all outlets | Looks orchestrated, hurts pickup. Each outlet rewrites the headline. |
| No assets ready at T-0 | Tier-2 outlets have a 60-minute window before they move on. Be ready. |

## VIII. Tier-1 pitch fallback ladder

If CoinDesk passes:
1. → The Block (Tim Copeland)
2. → Decrypt (Andrew Hayward)
3. → Bloomberg Crypto (Olga Kharif) — for original data piece only
4. → FinanceMagnates (Damian Chmiel)
5. → If all decline: skip the embargo, run a parallel-drop strategy with PANews / BlockBeats / Cointime as the anchor

A "no" from CoinDesk is normal, not a project-killing signal. It means we need to either (a) wait until we have a stronger news peg or (b) lead with the original data piece instead of the partnership announcement.

---

## IX. Post-embargo etiquette

- Send the embargo partner a personal thank-you note within 24 hours
- Do NOT share any traffic numbers back unless they ask
- Pitch them again only after they've published 2 unrelated pieces (avoid looking transactional)
- If the piece generates significant pushback, do NOT publicly disagree with the outlet
