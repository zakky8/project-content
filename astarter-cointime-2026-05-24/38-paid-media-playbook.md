# Paid Media Playbook

**For:** Phase 1 = earned-only. Phase 2+ = consider paid if earned reach plateaus.
**Purpose:** ready-to-deploy paid plan with pre-priced vendors so a paid push can launch in 48 hours if approved.

**Critical principle:** Paid media amplifies what's working organically. Paid CAN NOT save a story that isn't landing.

---

## I. When to deploy paid

**Green lights:**

- Day-7 retrospective shows we hit ≥target on impressions but ≤floor on conversions → paid retargeting fixes the funnel
- We're capturing share-of-voice in DeFAI category and want to extend the spike
- We have a high-conversion landing page already proven (>5% CR)
- A specific competitor announcement is about to drop and we need defensive lift
- A specific milestone (TGE, second alliance, beta launch) needs amplification

**Red lights — never deploy paid when:**

- Organic story is missing — paid won't save bad messaging
- Landing page conversion is unproven (paid sends traffic to a leaky bucket)
- Crisis comms is active (paid traffic to a crisis page is malpractice)
- Funnel analytics aren't instrumented (can't measure ROI)
- We don't have weekly review cadence in place

## IV. Budget recommendation by phase

### Phase 1 (T-0 to T+30) — earned-only

**Spend: $0**

Reasoning: We don't yet know what works organically. Spending paid before establishing organic baseline pollutes the data.

### Phase 2 (T+30 to T+90) — measured paid experiments

**Spend: $25–50K**

Allocation:
- $5K Twitter Ads test on top-performing organic thread
- $5K Milk Road or Decrypt Daily sponsorship
- $5K DePIN-specific podcast underwriting (DePIN Hub-adjacent)
- $5K LinkedIn Ads targeting institutional / investor audience
- $5K reserve for opportunistic competitor-defense or reactive spend
- $25K (if available) for Bankless newsletter primary placement

### Phase 3 (T+90 onward) — sustained paid as ROI proves

**Spend: $50–150K / quarter**

Allocation contingent on:
- Conversion data from Phase 2 (which channels actually moved the needle)
- Available milestones to amplify (TGE / second alliance / beta launch)
- Competitive landscape — are competitors going dark, going hard, or steady-state

## V. Measurement framework

### Per-channel attribution

For every paid placement, instrument:

- **Unique tracking URL** (UTM tagged: `utm_source`, `utm_medium`, `utm_campaign`)
- **Conversion tracking** (newsletter signup / Discord join / Quickstart completion / contact form)
- **Sentiment tracking** (replies, comments, quote-tweets — quality not just quantity)
- **Cost per qualified action** (not cost per impression — impressions are vanity)

### ROI calculation

For each campaign, after 14 days:

```
Total spend:               $______
Total qualified actions:   ______
Cost per qualified action: $______

Benchmark — anything under $50 / qualified action is healthy.
Anything over $200 / qualified action — kill the campaign.
```

### Comparison to organic baseline

Always compare paid performance to organic baseline for the same asset. If a tweet got 100K organic impressions and $1K of promo brought it to 250K — the promo "added" 150K impressions at $6.67 / 1K, which is fair but not extraordinary.

## VI. Creative best practices

### What works in paid creative

- **Sharp single-claim hook** (no compound claims)
- **One CTA** (read / try / join — never multiple)
- **Visual asset matches landing page** (no creative-page disconnect)
- **Specific number or metric** in headline if possible
- **No emojis stacking** (looks unprofessional in paid context)
- **Different copy by audience segment** (don't use one ad for all targeting)

### What kills paid creative

- Generic crypto imagery
- "Buy our token" framing
- Multi-claim ads ("DeFi + AI + DePIN + Web4 + ...")
- Unbranded creative
- Untrackable links
- Long-form copy (>40 words for Twitter, >80 for LinkedIn)

### Creative variants to test (always 3+ per campaign)

| Variant | Hook angle |
|---|---|
| A | Thesis-driven ("DeFi's next bottleneck isn't liquidity") |
| B | Partnership-driven ("Astarter × UXLINK — Web 4.0 alliance") |
| C | Concrete-benefit-driven ("Build agent-native apps with full settlement primitives") |

Test all three with equal budget for 72 hours, then concentrate spend on the winner.

## VII. Specific high-leverage paid play (when ready)

### "Defensive amplification" play

When Olas / Virtuals / Fetch announces a major milestone:

- Within 24 hours, Twitter Ads campaign targeting *their* followers with our DeFAI layer-map content
- $2K spend, 48-hour campaign
- Creative leads with comparison framing: "[Competitor] is great at [their layer]. We're solving the other layer."

This is a **comp-defense play** — relatively cheap, relatively high signal, captures attention exactly when category interest is elevated.

### "TGE amplification" play (post-token launch)

When AA TGE happens:

- Coordinated paid push across Twitter / LinkedIn / 3 newsletters
- $50K total spend over 7 days
- Creative drives to a dedicated `/token` landing page with utility explainer
- Goal: capture inbound interest at moment of peak organic search volume

### "Conference-adjacent" play

If we sponsor or speak at a major conference:

- Twitter Ads geo-targeted to attendees in week of conference
- Newsletter sponsor in newsletters readers attend that conference
- LinkedIn Ads to job-title attendees
- $10K coordinated spend
- Drives attendees to a special conference landing page with meeting-booking CTA

## VIII. What we should NEVER pay for

- Anonymous KOL retweets (CT shillers)
- "Promoted listing" on aggregators that don't audit (most pay-to-play directories)
- Press releases via wire services that have no editorial standard (most cheap wires)
- Discord / TG mass-message blasts
- Pop-up display ads
- Anything that requires not disclosing as paid

These don't just have poor ROI. They actively damage brand credibility.

---

## IX. Final principle

**Paid media is a force multiplier on a working strategy, not a substitute for one.**

If the organic story isn't landing in Phase 1, the answer is not to spend money to push a story that isn't resonating. The answer is to fix the story.

When paid is right, it can 3–5x the impact of an already-working campaign at acceptable cost. When paid is wrong, it can burn $100K with nothing to show for it.

The discipline: paid only enters the plan after organic has proven what works. Phase 1 = earned. Phase 2+ = paid IF the data says so.
