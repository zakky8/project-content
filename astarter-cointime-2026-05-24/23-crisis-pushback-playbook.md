# Crisis / Pushback Q&A Playbook

**Internal use.** For when an interview goes hostile, a regulator inquires, a competitor attacks, or a critic on CT goes after a weak point. The discipline: acknowledge what's true, contextualize what's incomplete, never defend what's indefensible, never lie. Reputation is recovered slowly; it's destroyed in a single bad answer.

---

## I. TVL / Traction Pushback

### Attack: "Your TVL is $113K. Why should anyone take this announcement seriously?"

**Don't:** dispute the number, attack the questioner, blame market conditions.
**Do:** acknowledge cleanly, reframe to thesis, name the right metric.

> *"That's an accurate number, and it's the right thing to ask about. TVL is the wrong measure of progress for what we're building right now. We're explicitly building rails ahead of the traffic — which means the right metrics for the next two quarters are (a) does the execution layer ship in production beta, (b) does at least one external team deploy agents on it, (c) does the multi-chain expansion land. If we hit those, we'll have something more meaningful to talk about than TVL. If we miss them, no amount of current TVL would have saved the thesis."*

---

### Attack: "Your token isn't even listed. This is pre-launch vaporware."

> *"The token is pre-launch — that's correct. What's not correct is that this is vaporware. The four-product stack has been running on Cardano since 2021. The infrastructure is operating, audited, and serving real users today. The token is the economic anchor we'll layer on top of an already-functional system, not the first thing we're shipping."*

---

### Attack: "You've been around since 2021 and you've barely shipped anything new. Why now?"

> *"Two answers. First, the stack we shipped between 2021 and 2024 is the foundation we're building on now — those four products are the reason this repositioning is credible, not in spite of them. Second, the timing of this announcement isn't arbitrary; it tracks the inflection point at which the agent economy moved from theory into measurable deployment. We're not late. We're early in the right direction."*

---

## II. Strategic / Positioning Pushback

### Attack: "DeFAI is just a buzzword. You're slapping AI on a DeFi project to chase a narrative."

> *"That's a fair concern given how many projects have done exactly that. Two things that distinguish what we're doing. First, the stack predates the narrative — the four products were built for DeFi use cases and are being repointed at agents, not rebuilt around them. Second, we name the same uncomfortable thing the skeptics name: most DeFAI is buzzword. The category will be defined by the projects that ship execution-layer infrastructure, not the ones that ship marketing. We're betting we're in the first category. The next two quarters will prove it either way."*

---

### Attack: "Cardano is dead for DeFi. Why are you still there?"

> *"Cardano is the substrate we started on and where the production stack runs. The multi-chain expansion roadmap reflects our view that DePIN and agent economies are inherently cross-chain — we will follow the flow of activity regardless of where it originates. Cardano stays as a foundational deployment, not as a constraint."*

---

### Attack: "Olas / Virtuals / Fetch are already winning this category. You're too late."

> *"Each of those teams is anchored at a different point in the agent stack. Olas at coordination. Virtuals at issuance. Fetch at deployment. None of them are anchored at the financial settlement layer where Astarter is positioning. The category isn't a single race; it's a stack with multiple winners at different layers. We're competing for the layer that the loudest projects have not yet claimed."*

---

## III. Token / Tokenomics Pushback

### Attack: "100M fixed supply is small. Are you sure that's not a structural problem?"

> *"Fixed-supply design is deliberate. Agents discount forward-supply expansion deterministically — variable-supply or inflationary token designs introduce uncertainty that machine counterparties handle worse than human ones. The fixed cap is a feature for the use case, not an oversight."*

---

### Attack: "When TGE? You keep saying 'soon' — that's a red flag."

> *"We've never said 'soon.' We've said we will announce TGE date and listing venues alongside the formal tokenomics publication, which is sequenced after the AA1→AA2 transition we completed in November. We don't pre-announce dates we're not ready to commit to. That's a discipline, not an evasion."*

---

### Attack: "The team is selling. The vesting unlocks are about to flood the market."

> *"Vesting schedules will be transparent in the formal tokenomics publication. The structure was designed with multi-year alignment — team unlocks track milestones, not calendar dates. The full schedule will be public before any TGE event."*

---

## IV. Team / Process Pushback

### Attack: "Nobody on the team has shipped anything at this scale before."

> *"The team has been operating production DeFi infrastructure on Cardano for four years. That's a meaningful operational record. We've never claimed expertise we don't have, and we've never overstated what we've shipped. What we're attempting next is harder than what we've done before — we're explicit about that. The right test is execution against the next two quarters, not credentials from before."*

---

### Attack: "Who's actually in charge over there? Where's the public team?"

> *"Founding entity is Astarter, joint venture with EMURGO since 2021. **[REQUIRES GK SIGN-OFF on whether to name current leadership publicly]**. We disclose what we're authorized to disclose."*

## V. Regulatory Pushback

### Attack (from regulator inquiry): "Your token appears to function as a security. How do you respond?"

**Critical:** This is the one conversation where the spokesperson should defer entirely.

> *"Thank you for the inquiry. We'll have our legal counsel respond directly to provide the full context required. Can you confirm the right address for that correspondence?"*

**Internal:** Immediately escalate to GK + legal. Do not improvise.

---

### Attack: "Are you compliant with [jurisdiction X] securities regulation?"

> *"Compliance posture is jurisdiction-specific and handled by our legal team. We'll route your specific question to the right contact rather than answer it imprecisely."*

---

## VI. Personal / Ad-Hominem Pushback

### Attack: "You're just doing PR for a project that's about to dump on retail."

**Do not:** get defensive, attack back, or moralize.

> *"I hear that read of the announcement. The way we'd ask people to evaluate us is by what we ship in the next 90 days — not by what we say in any single announcement. If the rails ship and external teams deploy on them, the thesis is real. If they don't, the criticism was right. We're comfortable being measured against that test."*

---

## VII. Disengagement Phrases (when a conversation should end)

| Situation | Phrase |
|---|---|
| Spiraling debate, no productive resolution | *"I think we've gone as far as I can productively go on this in this format. Happy to follow up offline if useful."* |
| Bad-faith engagement | *"I'm going to step back from this thread. Thanks for the engagement."* |
| Out-of-scope question | *"That's outside what I can responsibly answer right now. I'll route it to the right person."* |
| Hostile press during recorded event | *"That's a fair question. I want to give you a complete answer, which is going to require I follow up with specifics. Can we connect after this session?"* |

---

## VIII. Internal escalation triggers

Escalate to GK + legal immediately if:

- Regulator names Astarter or AA in any correspondence
- Major media outlet runs an unfavorable piece with specific allegations
- Class-action / lawsuit threat language appears in any forum
- High-profile CT figure (>100K followers) attacks with specific factual claims
- Counterparty (EMURGO, UXLINK) issues a public correction or distancing statement
- Anything involving fraud, manipulation, or material misstatement allegations

**Never handle alone. Never improvise legal language. Never delete the conversation evidence.**
