# Media FAQ — Anticipated Journalist Questions

**Internal use.** Drafted answers for spokesperson use across interviews, AMAs, and follow-up press queries. Every answer stays inside the message frame established in the Cointime piece. Where data is not public, the answer redirects rather than fabricates.

## I. Project & Positioning

### Q1. In one sentence, what is Astarter?
Astarter is a Cardano-native infrastructure platform building the settlement layer for the autonomous AI Agent economy — combining AI Agent, DePIN, and DeFAI primitives under a single coordinated stack.

### Q2. Why are you repositioning a DeFi project into an AI Agent infrastructure narrative? Isn't that just chasing a trend?
The four-product stack — Launchpad, DEX, Money Market, Tech Service Platform — has been live since 2021. Nothing about the technology is being repackaged for a narrative. What changed is the realization that the same primitives that power human-facing DeFi are exactly what autonomous agents will need to operate as financial actors. We're not pivoting; we're recognizing that the layer we already built has a much larger addressable market than DeFi alone.

### Q3. How is this different from Olas, Virtuals, Fetch.ai, or Giza?
Each of those projects has chosen a different anchor point in the agent stack. Virtuals optimizes for agent issuance velocity. Olas focuses on agent coordination. Fetch.ai operates one of the largest live agent deployment surfaces. Astarter is staking out the financial settlement layer underneath all of them — the unit of account, the credit primitives, the collateral plumbing that lets an agent function as an economic actor rather than just a software process.

### Q4. What does "settlement layer" actually mean in this context?
When Agent A pays Agent B for a service, the transaction has to clear deterministically — no chargebacks, no human arbitration, no improvisation when something breaks. Existing DeFi was built assuming a person can step in. Agents can't. Settlement infrastructure for agents means a set of financial primitives engineered from the ground up for deterministic machine-to-machine value transfer.

## II. The UXLINK Alliance

### Q5. Why UXLINK, specifically?
UXLINK brings one of the larger active social-graph distribution surfaces in Web3. Astarter brings the financial primitives. Most agent-economy projects pair an execution layer with another execution layer; we're pairing execution with distribution. That's the structural read on why this partnership matters more than its press footprint might suggest.

### Q6. Is this a financial partnership? Token swap? Joint venture?
This is a strategic alliance focused on integrated product development across our respective layers. We're not disclosing financial terms at this time.

### Q7. When does the integrated product ship?
We've indicated functional beta of the DeFAI execution layer within the next two quarters. More specifics will come in subsequent updates.

## III. Token & Tokenomics

### Q8. What is the AA token's max supply and current circulation?
Maximum supply is 100,000,000 AA, hard-capped. The AA1-to-AA2 transition completed in November 2025 cleared the foundational tokenomics work ahead of market launch. Specific circulating-supply figures and listing schedules will be released alongside the formal tokenomics publication.

### Q9. When is the TGE?
We haven't disclosed a date. We will when we're ready to commit to one publicly.

### Q10. Which exchanges?
Listing venues will be announced alongside the tokenomics publication. We don't comment on specific listings before they're confirmed.

### Q11. What does AA actually do across the stack?
Four roles, simultaneously: unit of account for agent-to-agent settlement; payment medium for DePIN compute resources accessed by agents; governance instrument for protocol parameters; and collateral primitive within the Money Market for agent-issued credit. The token is the economic anchor of the entire system, not a single-purpose utility.

## IV. Technical & Roadmap

### Q12. You're on Cardano today. Are you going multi-chain?
Yes. DePIN and agent economies are inherently cross-chain — an execution layer that doesn't follow the actual flow of activity will be structurally limited. We've signaled multi-chain expansion as a near-term priority. Specific target chains will be announced as deployments are ready.

### Q13. What's the audit posture? Have the contracts been audited?
We'll publish current audit status alongside the tokenomics release. For ongoing platform security, we follow standard production-grade audit cadence with our infrastructure partners.

### Q14. Where do I see actual agent traffic on Astarter today?
The current production stack serves DeFi traffic. The DeFAI execution layer — purpose-built for agent traffic — is the next major release, currently in pre-beta development. We'll share metrics once external production deployments are live.

## V. Risk & Skepticism

### Q15. Skeptical take — your TVL is small and your token isn't listed. Why should anyone care about this announcement?
That's a fair starting point. Two responses. First, this announcement is a thesis and an alliance, not a victory lap; we're explicit that the proof points come from the next two quarters, not from the present moment. Second, the projects that captured durable share in every prior cycle weren't the ones with the loudest current numbers — they were the ones whose infrastructure was already standing when the demand arrived. We're choosing to build the rails ahead of the traffic. If we're right about the timing, that's the right order to do it in. If we're wrong, no amount of TVL today would have changed the outcome.
