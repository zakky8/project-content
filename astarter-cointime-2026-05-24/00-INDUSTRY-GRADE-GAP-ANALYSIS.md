# Industry-Grade Gap Analysis

**Honest assessment of where this package sits vs. how category-defining launches actually work.**

If the goal is "send something good to Cointime" — v3 is more than enough. If the goal is **define the DeFAI category and own the narrative for the next 18 months** — v3 is missing the 20 things that separate agency-junior delivery from Sequoia / a16z / Stripe-grade execution.

---

## How real category-defining launches actually work

### Reference playbook 1 — Anthropic / OpenAI model launch
- 6 weeks of internal alignment before any external comm
- Exclusive first-look offered to **one** Tier-1 outlet (NYT, WSJ, The Verge) under embargo
- Coordinated multi-outlet drop at T+0 with sequenced second-day pieces in lower-tier outlets
- Pre-briefed analyst community (Gartner, Forrester) 48 hours ahead
- Pre-recorded video assets ready for embed in every published piece
- Same-day developer documentation goes live
- Same-day pricing page goes live
- Day +1: founders do podcast tour (5–8 booked in advance)
- Day +7: technical deep-dive blog from the engineering team
- Day +14: customer case study
- Day +30: retrospective metrics post showing adoption

### Reference playbook 2 — Stripe brand launch
- Brand book is 80+ pages, every color in hex/RGB/CMYK/Pantone, type sizes pre-calculated for every breakpoint
- Every asset audited against the brand book before publication
- Internal style guide for support, sales, partnerships — same voice everywhere
- Co-marketing toolkit for every named partner — partner can self-serve
- All claims pre-cleared by legal with versioned approval log

### Reference playbook 3 — a16z State of Crypto report
- **Original data** is the entire reason it gets republished — proprietary analysis, not borrowed numbers
- 30+ chart-quality visualizations, all sourced and downloadable
- Released under exclusive embargo to one outlet (Bloomberg historically), then broad
- Pre-built one-tweet versions of every key chart for organic spread
- Sponsored by warmly amplifying every Tier-1 KOL in the category
- Becomes the citation source for the next 12 months of category articles

### Reference playbook 4 — Coinbase listing announcement
- Multi-language ships day-of: EN, ZH, KO, JP, ES, PT, RU, TR, AR, VN, ID
- Each region has a local PR contact and pre-vetted local outlets
- Compliance language pre-cleared in every jurisdiction
- Region-specific assets (KR uses KaTalk threads; CN uses WeChat moments; etc.)
- Same global narrative, region-specific channel execution

## The 20 gaps in v3

### Tier S — must close to be industry-grade

| # | Gap | Why it matters | What real PR shops have |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **No stakeholder map** | "Send to Cointime" isn't a strategy. Real launches name 50+ journalists, 30+ KOLs, 20+ podcasts, 25+ partners with relationship state and outreach sequence. | Tiered list in CRM with last-touch, beat preference, recent-coverage notes. |
| 2 | **No enforceable brand guidelines** | Design philosophy is aesthetic — guidelines are enforceable. Pentagram delivers a 60-page brand book that anyone in the company can use to produce on-brand assets. | Hex / RGB / CMYK / Pantone per color; type scale per breakpoint; logo do/don't grid; voice rules. |
| 3 | **No measurement framework** | "I sent the article" is not a result. Real comms tracks share-of-voice, sentiment, syndication map, message penetration, ICP overlap. | Weekly dashboard in BI tool or spreadsheet with named KPIs and targets. |
| 4 | **No embargo / multi-outlet drop strategy** | One-outlet drops cap your reach at that outlet's audience. Real launches use exclusive embargo → broad drop → second-day pieces to amplify 5–10x. | Sequenced outlet plan, embargo language, exclusive-pitch templates, drop-day checklist. |
| 5 | **No internal comms** | If the Astarter team itself isn't aligned, every team member becomes a leak risk. Real launches do all-hands 7 days before, team FAQ, support-team training. | All-hands deck, internal memo, team FAQ, role-specific talking points. |
| 6 | **No community / holder comms** | AA holders, ISPO participants, money-market users find out from press — they should hear from the project first. Bad sequencing burns the most loyal users. | Direct community email/TG/Discord before press drop. |
| 7 | **No partner co-marketing kit** | UXLINK can't amplify what they don't have ready-to-use assets for. Real co-marketing ships a joint asset kit the partner self-serves from. | Joint one-pager, joint comparison sheet, partner-branded variant of hero PNG, joint social copy. |
| 8 | **No developer marketing** | For an *infrastructure* pitch with no developer-facing materials, the technical audience reads it as marketing fluff. Real infra launches ship docs + Quickstart + architecture diagram day-of. | Docs site, Quickstart, architecture diagram, README, RFC template, Discord developer channel charter. |
| 9 | **No competitive intelligence operations** | We did a one-shot competitive scan in research. Real PR runs *weekly* comp watch — what are Olas/Virtuals/Fetch saying, which topics are they ceding, where are they vulnerable. | Weekly comp report template + alert rules. |
| 10 | **No multilingual beyond CN** | Crypto adoption is regional. KR / JP / ES / PT are first-class markets. Shipping only EN+CN concedes 40%+ of the addressable audience to competitors. | Region-pack with locale-specific copy + channel mapping. |
| 11 | **No original data / research** | Press releases get aggregated. Original analysis with proprietary data gets cited for 12 months. Messari and Delphi don't get republished because of their poster design. | One thought-leadership piece per quarter anchored to original data analysis. |
| 12 | **No SEO / discoverability strategy** | A Cointime article generates a spike. SEO + landing page optimization generates a 12-month tail. | Keyword brief, structured data, meta strategy, content plan for the long tail. |
| 13 | **No post-launch retrospective** | If you don't close the loop, you can't optimize Phase 2. Real launches publish a Day-7 + Day-30 retrospective internally. | Retrospective template with named metrics and decision points. |
| 14 | **No paid media playbook** | Even if Phase 1 is earned-only, the *capability* to deploy paid in 48 hours if needed is what separates pro shops. | Pre-priced playbook by channel with vendor contacts. |
