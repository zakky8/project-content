Hi,

A short note on something we've been quietly building toward for a while, and a strategic update on where we're going next.

**The short version**

The defining trade of the 2026 cycle is no longer about which chain wins liquidity. It's about who builds the settlement layer for the first generation of economic actors that aren't human.

Autonomous AI agents have crossed the line from theory into deployment. Olas-powered agents are now a meaningful share of activity on Polymarket. Virtuals has crossed $2B in agent-linked trading volume. Fetch.ai's ASI:One has 150K+ deployed agents. The agent economy is real.

But everyone building inside this category is converging on the same realization: agents don't need better models. They need better rails. Existing DeFi was built for humans behind wallets. Agents can't improvise when settlement breaks.

That bottleneck is the slot Astarter has been quietly positioning toward for the last 24 months.

**What we announced**

On May 15 we announced our strategic alliance with UXLINK — one of the larger Web3 social-growth networks. The structural read: we bring the execution layer. They bring the distribution layer. It's the first credible attempt the category has seen to pair both in one stack.

**What we're actually building**

Three things, under one coordinated thesis:

→ AI Agent — the actor
→ DePIN — the substrate it runs on
→ DeFAI — the financial layer it operates within

Most projects in this space cover one. We're committed to all three.

The AA token anchors the system economically. Fixed supply of 100,000,000, with four simultaneous roles across the stack: unit of account for agent settlement, payment medium for DePIN compute, governance instrument, and collateral primitive for agent credit.
