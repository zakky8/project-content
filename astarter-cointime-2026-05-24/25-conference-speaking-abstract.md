## Master Abstract (350 words — long form)

**Title:** *The Settlement Layer of the AI Agent Economy: Why DeFi's Next Bottleneck Is Not Liquidity*

**Track suggestion:** Infrastructure · AI x Crypto · DeFi

**Format suggestion:** 25-minute keynote OR 45-minute fireside (preferred) OR 60-minute panel as moderator

**Abstract:**

The defining trade of the 2026 crypto cycle is not about which chain wins the next liquidity war, nor which Layer-2 captures the next wave of users. It is about who builds the settlement substrate for the first generation of economic actors that are not human.

Autonomous AI agents have crossed the line from speculative narrative into measurable on-chain activity. Olas-powered agents account for a non-trivial share of activity on prediction markets. Virtuals Protocol has crossed two billion dollars in agent-linked trading volume. Fetch.ai's ASI:One framework reports over one hundred fifty thousand deployed agents and counting. Yet the financial infrastructure these agents will require to operate as true economic actors — deterministic settlement, predictable fees, permissionless composability, trust-minimized clearing without human arbitration — does not yet exist at scale.

This talk introduces the emerging category designed to address that gap: **DeFAI** — the intersection of decentralized finance and autonomous AI execution. Drawing on a four-year operating record building DeFi infrastructure on Cardano and a recent strategic alliance integrating distribution and execution layers under a single coordinated stack, the speaker will walk attendees through (a) why agent-native settlement infrastructure represents one of the largest under-claimed opportunities of the cycle, (b) the structural reasons most current AI agent projects are anchored at the wrong layer of the stack, and (c) what the next twenty-four months of category development will likely look like.

The session will name peer projects explicitly (Olas, Virtuals, Fetch.ai, Giza) and discuss the differentiated structural positions each is taking. Attendees will leave with a framework for evaluating AI Agent infrastructure projects on their settlement-layer alignment, and a clearer view of where capital and integration partnerships are most likely to concentrate over the coming year.
